# Late Game Roadmap

Design vision. The late game is a chain of science tiers, like a long science
tree that branches and then converges to one final science. Each tier is a new
science pack you make, and making it unlocks the next batch of features. You
always have a clear next science to chase, and each one changes how you build.

Aspirational and v0.2 and beyond. Nothing here is built yet. Original, not SE.
The identity stays the same. Endless procedural planets, each forcing a
different way to build, and you plan self running colonies rather than pilot a
ship.

## Shape
Vanilla science leads into a JJtorio chain. Rocket, then orbital, then a
branching row of one science per planet paradigm, then converging tiers, then a
final science. The tree branches wide in the middle and narrows to the end.

## The chain

### Tier 0. Vanilla base
The six vanilla packs plus the rocket silo. This is 0.1.x today.

### Tier 1. Rocket Science. Feature: the first step off world
Gate: the rocket silo tech. Make it from rocket parts and early space materials
on the ground. Unlocks sending cargo up and reaching orbit.

### Tier 2. Orbital Science. Feature: build in orbit
Gate: reach orbit. Make it only in orbit, from lifted materials. Unlocks
orbital construction and the survey of nearby worlds.

### Tier 3. The planet sciences, a branching row of six
One per planet paradigm. You make each by operating a colony under that
constraint, so you must hold diverse worlds. They branch from orbital science
and can be researched in any order.
- Vacuum Science. Airless worlds where heat cannot dissipate.
- Cryo Science. Frozen worlds where everything sheds heat too fast.
- Magma Science. Volcanic worlds with free heat but corrosion.
- Tide Science. Oceanic worlds with little land and floating builds.
- Gravity Science. High or low gravity logistics.
- Verdant Science. Fertile worlds and biological processing.
Each unlocks the tools to tame its paradigm.

### Tier 4. Resonance Science. Feature: the logistics web
Gate: at least three planet sciences. Make it from a balanced multi world
freight network. Unlocks bulk interworld logistics and colony autonomy.

### Tier 5. Core Science. Feature: deep extraction
Gate: Resonance Science plus a dense crust colony. Make it from refined Core
Tap fragments. Unlocks the Core Tap line and exotic materials.

### Tier 6. Exotic Science. Feature: materials with no vanilla analogue
Gate: Core Science. Make it from cross planet fragment blends. Unlocks advanced
buildings and the force sciences below.

### Tier 7. The force sciences, a short branch
Earned by using a paradigm as a tool, not just surviving it. Gravitic Science,
Thermal Science, and Flux Science. They unlock megastructure parts and light
terraforming.

### Tier 8. Stellar Science. Feature: harness the star
Gate: the force sciences. Make it from stellar collectors. Unlocks system scale
power and large megastructures.

### Tier 9. Ascendant Science. Feature: the frontier
Gate: Stellar Science plus compound paradigm colonies. Make it from several
extreme world outputs at once. Unlocks the hardest worlds and the endgame build.

### Tier 10. Convergence Science. The final science, the white sphere
Gate: Ascendant Science plus a broad autonomous empire. Make it from the whole
network running as one closed loop. Unlocks the victory build, either a Genesis
Forge that seeds a brand new world you design, or a quieter Long Signal ending,
plus infinite post victory research.

## How this drives features
Every mid and late game feature lands as the payoff of a science tier, so there
is always a next target. Planets, orbit, core tap, colonies, and megastructures
each enter the game as a tier unlock rather than all at once.

## Build order for us
Ship one tier at a time as a vertical slice. Rocket Science first, since it is
the bridge from 0.1.x. Then Orbital Science. Then one planet science to prove
the paradigm pattern. Only widen after each slice works in game.
